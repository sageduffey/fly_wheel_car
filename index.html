Fly Wheel Car Deconstruction

Sage Duffey & Anna Gelich

A Fly Wheel Car is a mechanical toy that is powered by stored energy as the car is pulled back.

Construction can be broken down into three phases of construction
1. Weighted Axle
2. Gear Box
3. Exterior Shell

Deconstructed Car Labeled

Weighted Axle

The Weighted Axle consisted of five pieces that sit within the gear box. As the car is drawn back, energy is stored.

A1.Core Rod - The Axle of this piece, it fits within the Gear Box
A2.Wheel Weights - 4 metalic weights
A3.Axle Gear - Fits into two symentrical gears, B1
A4.Axle Spacer - Keeps the gear in the correct spot to fit into Gear Box
A5.Axle Spring - Keeps the pieces in the correct position
A6.Axle Cap - Finishes the Axle

Axle Deconstructed

Gear Box

The Gear Box transfered to and from the Wheels and Weighted Axle. The gear system is symmetrical, which eleminates any issues of

B1.Primary Gear - Connects to A3 and B2. This piece consists of 2 gears, larger and smaller, which increases the rotation speed of B2.
B2.Secondary Gear - Connects to B1 and B3.
B3.Tertiary Gear - Connects to B2, is fixed to Wheel Axles.
B4.Wheel Axles - Intersects the Gear Box, is fixed to B3 and B6.
B5.Gear Box - Two plastic pieces that hold together the gear ratio, snap fit.
B6.Wheels - 4 pieces that are fixed to B4, wheel axles.

Exterior Shell

The Exterior Shell is the commercial envelope that

C1.Gear Box Shell - 2 pieces that fit around B4, Gear Box, connected with 1 C5.
C2.Primary Car Shell x 2 - Fits together with 4 C5
C3.Interior Piece One - Snap fits into C2, represents the hood of the car
C4.Interior Piece Two - Snap fits into C3, represents the cab of the car
C5.Screws x5 - Connects C1 and C2, independantly.
